ABOUT:

Thesis presentation for my Bachelor of Science in Art and Design degree from MIT. The presentation was a performance in disguise, of a series of carefully crafted glitches and fake troubleshooting that gradually escalated in severity to question our perception of error.


What is noise? What is signal? Who gets to decide?


Watch the full perforamnce here (and my professors being completely fooled :))

TECHNICAL INFO:

The presentation was actually a live audiovisual system I built and ran on Touchdesigner, which I used to display images of slides and trigger various glitching animations on them. With the hardware, I made my own thesis VGA cable which I tested on the AV system beforehand. There was an AV team for thesis presentations who were assigned to help in the case of technical troubles. I specifically told them that I did NOT need help.
